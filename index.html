CrashSight: A Phase-Aware, Infrastructure-Centric Video Benchmark for Traffic Crash Scene Understanding

Rui Gan1, Pei Li2, Junyi Ma1, Xingyou Yang1, Sikai Chen1, Bin Ran1
1University of Wisconsin–Madison    2University of Wyoming
GitHub Code Paper (arXiv) Dataset

Abstract

Cooperative autonomous driving requires traffic scene understanding from both vehicle and infrastructure perspectives. While vision-language models (VLMs) show strong general reasoning capabilities, their performance in safety-critical scenarios remains insufficiently evaluated due to the ego-vehicle focus of existing benchmarks. To bridge this gap, we present CrashSight, a large-scale vision-language benchmark for roadway crash understanding using real-world roadside camera data. We benchmark 8 state-of-the-art VLM configurations and show that, despite strong scene description capabilities, current models struggle with temporal and causal reasoning in safety-critical scenarios.

Error Taxonomy and Failure Analysis

Our transition analysis across 1,962 test samples identifies three compounding factors restricting current VLM performance on crash mechanics: insufficient visual token budgets, quantization overhead, and frozen visual encoders. The proposed benchmark provides a standardized evaluation framework to overcome these limitations for infrastructure-assisted perception in V2X-enabled cooperative driving.
